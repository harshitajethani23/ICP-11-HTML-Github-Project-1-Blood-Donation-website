Donor Eligibility Guidelines



Read all the guidelines before donate the blood.These requirements are essential for blood donation.

Weight: The minimum weight to donate blood varies by country and the type of donation.In general, your weight should be at least 45kg.
Weight requirements by country

  • India:
    • you must weigh at least 45kg(99Ibs) and have a hemoglobin level of atleast 12.5 gDL.
  • World Health Organization (WHO):
    • You should must weigh at least 50 kg (110Ibs)

Antibiotics: You can donate blood after finishing a course of antibiotics for a bacterial or viral infection.you can donate blood on the day you take your last pill. If you took antibiotics by injection,you can donate 10 days after your last injection.
Other medications: vitamins,hormones,birth control pills,thyroid medications,aspirin fertility drugs,non-steroid anti-inflammatory and pain medications are all acceptable.

Health: You must be in good health and feeling well on the day of donation.Prospective donors should eat a full meal within four hours of donating, avoiding fried, fatty foods and whole dairy products.These criteria include:

  • Age:
    • You should be between 18 and 65 years old.
  • Blood pressure:
    • you should have a blood pressure within a normal range.
  • Pulse rate:
    • You should have a pulse rate within a normal range.
  • Temperature:
    • You should have a normal oral temperature.
  • Mental and physical health:
    • You should be in good mental and physical health.
  • Infections:
    • You should not have a cold,flu,sore throat,stomach bug,or any other infection.

Heart Conditions: An angiogram is acceptable if results were normal.Angioplasty,stent,or heart attack are acceptable after 6 months if stable and no activity restrictions.Heart surgery is a 12 month deferral if stable and no restrictions.

  • Heart attack:
    • You should wait at least six months after a heart attack before donating blood.You cant donate if you have had more than one heart attack.
  • Heart failure:
    • You should not donate blood because it can make your condition worse.
  • Heart surgery:
    • You should wait six months after surgery and have no other symptoms before donating.
  • Abnormal heartbeat:
    • You cannot donate blood if you need medication to control an abnormal heartbeat.
  • Symptoms:
    • You cannot donate blood if you have symptoms like dizziness,shortness of breath,weakness,or extreme tiredness.
